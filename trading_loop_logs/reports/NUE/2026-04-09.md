This high leverage poses a significant risk, particularly relevant if macroeconomic conditions shift unfavorably.

**4. Countering the Bull's Argument: Earnings Growth Isn’t Enough**
The bulls argue that Nucor's strong revenue and earnings growth metrics, reported at **8.6% YoY** and **34.2% YoY**, respectively, justify its valuation. While these growth rates are impressive, they may not offset the valuation risks when the company is fundamentally over-leveraged and struggling for cash flow. The prospects of earnings growth are clouded by the expectations for a substantial quarter; a disappointing report could negate the bullish thesis completely. Hence, even if growth metrics remain strong, they are not sufficient to recommend purchasing or holding shares in a context of such high valuation and financial strain.

**5. Conviction Score**
**Bear conviction: 8/10**  
In summary, the combination of significant overvaluation, bearish technical indicators signaling a potential price correction, ongoing free cash flow issues, and high leverage creates a precarious situation for potential investors.
