The **current P/E ratio** of 13.68 is not overly alarming but the **forward P/E** of 8.79 suggests suppressed future earnings expectations amidst high leverage. While bulls argue CMC is positioned strongly due to its revenue growth of **21.5% YoY** and upcoming infrastructure spending, it is important to note that **revenue growth has not translated into profitability** improvements as reflected in recent earnings misses. Even with the anticipated demand spike, if CMC fails to effectively leverage this growth to enhance margins, the risk of losses will far outweigh the potential benefits. Moreover, the low **short float of 5.9%** minimizes the likelihood of a short squeeze, not providing a safety net for bearish investors if the stock begins to decline further.

#### 5. **Conviction Score**
Bear conviction: **8/10**. The combination of disappointing earnings history, technical indicators signaling weakness, and insufficient leverage to capitalize on potential growth puts **CMC at significant risk** for a further price decline, making it a poor investment decision in the short term.
